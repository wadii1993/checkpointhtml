A personal career path is one that you create for yourself. It's based on a self-assessment of your skills, interests, hobbies, values, and passions. A personal career path can lay the foundation of your career without being restricted to one organization. If you’re ready to create your career path, start by making a list of the skills you’re good at, followed by your interests and hobbies. Then write down the things that matter most to you.

Look at everything on your list and think about the industries that could benefit from your talent. For example, if you’re great with children, consider a career path in education. If you prefer healthcare more than teaching, look into a career path for pediatric nurses or doctors. You can also take a career path quiz or a personality assessment to find out the best careers for you. With a personal career path, you have complete control of your career development. A personal career path may require more work, but it can be very rewarding. You have to take the initiative to find resources that help with your career development. Research the things that will prepare you for the next job. Network with others in your industry even if they work for a different company.
